**1. The Northpointe mischaracterization survives in three places, including the abstract.** The letter says "(a) We corrected the abstract and body." The correction landed in §2.2 and §4.3 (both now correctly say the defence was threshold predictive parity, "not full per-score calibration"). So the paper currently asserts the per-score claim three times and refutes it twice. This is the reviewer's most concrete factual correction; finding it un-landed in the abstract is the fastest route to a second revision round. Fix all three to the threshold-PPV phrasing.

**2. Letter item 8's tail is not in the manuscript.** The response says "We also note k-NN is not assumption-free (matching set, metric, scaling, k), and interpret k-stability cautiously." I can't find either. §3.2 still says k-NN "makes no functional-form assumption" with no counterweight that the assumptions are *shifted* to the matching set, metric, scaling, and k; and the scale-invariance is still called "itself a substantive finding" (§3.2) and "replicated across six neighbourhood scales" (§4.6) with no version of the reviewer's caution that persistent stability is equally consistent with persistent omitted-variable imbalance. Two sentences fix this — one in §3.2, one where scale-invariance is celebrated.

**3. The abstract wasn't narrowed at all.** Body: "diagnostic," "screening signal," "conditional matched-comparison." Abstract: "a matched-pair, case-level *test* of whether an algorithm treats individually-similar defendants similarly… The answer for COMPAS is no." Two specific repairs: "individually-similar" should become "similar on selected legitimate predictors" (this is the reviewer's point 3 verbatim — the intro fixed it, the abstract didn't), and the categorical "the answer is no" needs the conditional register, e.g., "COMPAS exhibits a robust case-level disparity: matched defendants…" The abstract should also gain one sentence on the robustness analysis, since it's now the revision's evidentiary centerpiece and the response letter's own headline.

**4. A technical error nobody caught: the counterfactual-fairness limit is backwards.** §5.3 says "LBI → 0 in the limit of perfect counterfactual fairness." Under perfect counterfactual fairness the protected attribute has no effect on outputs, so cross-group matched gaps approach same-group gaps and **LBI → 1**, not 0. LBI → 0 would describe an algorithm whose cross-group neighbours receive *identical* scores while same-group neighbours differ — a bizarre pathology, not fairness. Since the reviewer didn't flag it, fixing it silently in this pass is free; leaving it invites a new objection from a sharper second reader.
