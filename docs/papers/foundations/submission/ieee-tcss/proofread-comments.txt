 several proofreading and consistency issues I would fix before submitting.

Highest-priority manuscript fixes

The biggest issue is that the manuscript still contains revision-facing language inside the article itself. For TCSS, the manuscript should read as a standalone paper, not as a revision document.

Change these:

Abstract sentence to revise:

“The revision additionally makes the model self-contained…”

Suggested replacement:

“The paper also makes the model self-contained…”

Section I.A heading sentence to revise:

“This paper makes six revised contributions:”

Suggested replacement:

“This paper makes six contributions:”

Conclusion opening to revise:

“This paper revises and tests a geometric model…”

Suggested replacement:

“This paper presents and tests a geometric model…”

These occur in the abstract, contributions section, and conclusion of the revised manuscript. The paper otherwise emphasizes that it is self-contained and no longer relies on companion papers, so this revision-facing wording undercuts that framing.

Important consistency issue: companion-paper language remains

The response to reviewers says you removed the working-paper dependencies and that the manuscript no longer depends on unpublished companion work. But the manuscript still says:

“We develop this reframing of bounded rationality as admissible heuristic truncation in a companion theoretical paper…”

That sentence appears in Section VII.B and directly conflicts with the “self-contained” claim in the response. I would either remove it or recast it without referring to a companion paper.

Suggested replacement:

“More broadly, the geometric vocabulary identified here may provide the kind of structured search space required for heuristic models of bounded rationality, although developing that computational interpretation is beyond the scope of the present empirical paper.”

Response-to-reviewers consistency fixes

Your response letter is clear and thorough, but I found two places where it appears to overstate what is actually in the manuscript.

First, the response says Section V.D adds “sample-size-weighted MAE,” but the manuscript reports per-subset MAE, Ruggeri-subset MAE, CI coverage, and Andersen AIC/LRT results. I did not see an actual sample-size-weighted MAE reported in the manuscript.

Change “sample-size-weighted MAE” to “per-subset MAE and high-N Ruggeri-subset MAE,” unless you add the weighted MAE calculation to the manuscript.

Second, the response repeatedly says “AIC/BIC,” but the manuscript text I saw reports AIC and likelihood-ratio tests; BIC is mentioned generally but no BIC value appears in the Andersen result.

Either add BIC values to Section VII.A or revise the response letter to say “AIC and likelihood-ratio tests.”

Style and presentation suggestions

In the manuscript byline, “Andrew H. Bond, Senior Lecturer” may not match IEEE style. IEEE typically uses author names in the byline and puts affiliation details in the footnote, not job titles. I would consider changing the byline to just “Andrew H. Bond” unless TCSS specifically permits titles.

The affiliation line shows “San Jose´ State University,” which looks like an encoding/LaTeX accent issue. Use either “San José State University” if your LaTeX setup handles it cleanly, or “San Jose State University” to avoid a garbled accent.

In the response letter, the opening says “We thank the reviewer,” singular, while the document is titled “Response to Reviewers.” If there was only one reviewer, that is fine; otherwise use “reviewers” throughout.
